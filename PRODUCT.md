# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

South Florida homeowners and property managers in two distinct modes:

1. **Emergency mode**: pipe burst at 2am, flooding after a storm, mold discovered before a sale. High anxiety, needs immediate clarity, calls before they read. Bilingual — many are Spanish-dominant.
2. **Planning mode**: scheduled restoration work, post-insurance project, impact window installation. Has time to research, compares options, reads the page.

Both audiences exist. Emergency mode dominates conversion volume; planning mode drives project size and margin.

## Product Purpose

EnviroPro USA is a 36-year-old South Florida restoration contractor. The website exists to generate two types of conversions: emergency calls (24/7 hotline) and project lead forms (water, mold, fire, hurricane, impact windows). Secondary goal: establish enough credibility to justify premium pricing over generic franchise competitors.

Success = phone rings and forms submit. The site is not a brand story — it is a conversion machine with a premium finish.

## Brand Personality

Confiable · Urgente · Profesional

Voice: authoritative but human. Talks like a skilled contractor who has seen it all, not a corporate call center. Direct. No filler. In Spanish: same tone, not translated — natively bilingual.

Emotional goal: when a homeowner lands at 2am with water on the floor, they should feel *relief* within 3 seconds. "These people will handle it."

## Anti-references

- **HomeAdvisor / Angi contractor templates**: stock photos of smiling workers in hard hats, orange/yellow palette, generic trust badges, "Get Free Quotes" hero. Cheap-looking, commodity positioning.
- **Medical/clinical white**: aseptic, cold. Wrong sector signals.
- Over-animated agency sites that feel clever rather than capable.

## Design Principles

1. **Emergency-first hierarchy**: the call CTA must be reachable within one thumb tap at all times. Urgency is never sacrificed for aesthetics.
2. **Premium over generic**: outclass the HomeAdvisor aesthetic on every surface. Better typography, better spacing, more editorial. The design itself signals "we are not the cheap option."
3. **Credentials as evidence, not claims**: IICRC, 36 years, insurance direct billing — stated as facts alongside context, not shouted as badges.
4. **Bilingual by design**: Spanish is not a translated afterthought. Same quality, same hierarchy, same editorial care on `/es/` pages.
5. **Calm authority in crisis**: design communicates capability without manufacturing panic. Color and copy should reduce anxiety, not amplify it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Minimum contrast 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Keyboard-navigable. Semantic HTML throughout. Emergency phone links must be touch-target compliant (min 44×44px). Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`.
